# Creativity

## What Is Creativity?

**Margaret Boden's three modes**:
- **Combinational creativity**: Unfamiliar combinations of familiar ideas
- **Exploratory creativity**: Systematically exploring a conceptual space (~97% of human creativity)
- **Transformational creativity**: Changing the rules of the conceptual space itself

Also: **H-creativity** (historically new to all humanity) vs **P-creativity** (new to the individual).

**Standard definition** (Runco & Jaeger, 2012): Requires both **novelty** and **usefulness/appropriateness**. Pure novelty without value isn't creativity — it's noise.

**Keith Sawyer**: Creativity is fundamentally collaborative and contextual, not the product of a lone genius.

## Original vs Derived

- **Original (H-creative)**: Historically unprecedented — Einstein, Picasso, Bach
- **Derived (P-creative)**: Novel to the individual but not to history

**Csikszentmihalyi's systems model**: Creativity isn't a property of the individual alone but of the interaction between person, domain, and field. What counts as "derived" depends on who's evaluating.

## Can Machines Be Creative?

**Boden**: AI can produce outputs fitting criteria for all three types. But "real" creativity requires autonomy, intentionality, valuation, emotion, and consciousness.

**Functionalism**: If an AI produces outputs indistinguishable from human creativity, there may be no meaningful distinction between "real" and "simulated."

**Recent research**:
- **Huang (2025)**: Goldilocks effect — moderate human-AI collaboration maximizes creativity. Too much AI decreases it.
- **Ivcevic & Grandinetti (2024)**: AI augments creativity most effectively at mini-c and little-c levels, with diminishing returns at Pro-C and Big-C.
- **arXiv review (2025)**: AI excels at exploratory creativity but struggles with transformational creativity.

## Role of Constraint in Creativity

Constraints **enable** creativity more than they restrict it.

- Monet restricted himself to same subjects under varying light
- Picasso limited his palette during Blue and Rose periods
- Haught-Tromp: participants with tight constraints produced MORE creative outputs than unconstrained participants

**Mechanism**: With unlimited choices, the brain defaults to satisficing. Constraints eliminate obvious solutions, forcing deeper search into the solution space.

## Brain During Creative Insight

**Four types** (Dietrich, 2004):
- Deliberate cognitive (scientific reasoning)
- Deliberate emotional (poetry)
- Spontaneous cognitive ("eureka" moments)
- Spontaneous emotional (musical inspiration)

**Neural networks**:
- **Default Mode Network (DMN)**: Active during spontaneous idea generation, mind-wandering
- **Executive Control Network (ECN)**: Active during deliberate, focused creative work
- **2025 jazz improvisation study**: Deep creativity involves DMN + ECN + salience network simultaneously

**Charles Limb (2008)**: Jazz improvisation inhibited dorsolateral PFC (executive control) while activating medial PFC (self-expression). Creativity isn't an "on switch" — it's a complex, distributed dance.

## Creativity and Consciousness

**Baumeister et al. (2014)**: Creativity declines sharply when consciousness is preoccupied. Requires interactive collaboration of conscious and unconscious processes.

**Dietrich (2004)**: Creative insights occur in consciousness — require PFC working memory buffer.

**Sam Coleman**: Creativity should be defined through phenomenal consciousness. Without phenomenal consciousness, a system cannot be creative regardless of output quality.

**Bergsonian position**: Genuine novelty requires the lived, temporal experience of a conscious agent — cannot be reduced to mechanism.

**Emerging view**: Dual-process model — unconscious generation (DMN) produces candidate ideas, but conscious processing (ECN, PFC) is required to evaluate, edit, and integrate.
