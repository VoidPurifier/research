# Random World Facts

## Venezuela's Eternal Lightning Storm
- Catatumbo Lightning over Lake Maracaibo
- Strikes up to 28 times per minute
- Occurs 140-260 nights per year
- Sailors used it for centuries as a natural lighthouse

## Blood Falls, Antarctica
- Rust-red waterfall pours from Taylor Glacier into a white landscape
- Color comes from iron-rich water from an ancient subglacial lake
- Microbes survive without sunlight or oxygen
- Conditions similar to possible extraterrestrial habitats

## Canada Has More Lakes Than the Rest of the World Combined
- Canada alone holds more lakes than all other countries on Earth put together
- Many are so large they're classified as enclosed seas

## The Sahara Was Once Green
- One of Earth's harshest environments was a lush, habitable landscape
- Had lakes, rivers, and human settlements as recently as 6,000 years ago

## Salar de Uyuni, Bolivia
- World's largest salt flat, spans over 10,000 km²
- During rainy season, a thin layer of water transforms it into a perfect natural mirror
- Creates surreal illusion of walking on the sky
