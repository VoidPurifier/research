# Millennium Prize Problems — Current Status

## 1. P vs NP — Still Wide Open
- Expert consensus: no viable approach exists, proof unlikely in anyone's lifetime
- Relativization, natural proofs, and algebrization barriers rule out major strategies
- Stephen Wolfram (Jan 2026) explored restricted forms of computational irreducibility — not a path to proof but gives intuition about why it's so hard

## 2. Riemann Hypothesis — No Progress, Few Attempts
- "There's no news to share" — leading analytic number theorists barely working on it
- Confirmed for first ~10 trillion zeros numerically
- Maynard–Guth (2024): best breakthrough in decades — proved no zero is *too far* from critical line
- Any proof will require fundamentally new mathematics

## 3. Hodge Conjecture — Known in Low Dimensions, Hard in 4+
- True for Hodge classes of degree 1 and 2, varieties of dimension ≤ 3
- 2026: explicit obstruction result showing current geometric techniques are insufficient
- No counterexample exists, but gap between proven and conjectured remains vast

## 4. Navier–Stokes Existence & Smoothness — Active Area
- 2D: fully solved
- 3D weak solutions: exist globally, but uniqueness and smoothness unknown
- Non-uniqueness discovered (Albritton–Brué–Colombo 2022, Hou–Wang–Yang 2025)
- Tao (2014/2016): blowup for *averaged* version, showing no purely structural obstruction
- 2026: claimed proof for axisymmetric-with-swirl case

## 5. Yang–Mills Existence & Mass Gap — Most Active
- 3D: solved by Chatterjee
- Large-N limit: mass gap proved (Cao, Chatterjee et al. 2025)
- Serafini et al. (June 2025): claimed constructive proof for SU(3) on ℝ⁴ — under review
- Balaban programme completions ongoing (2026)
- *Nature Reviews Physics* (Jan 2026): "significant progress, but full continuum result remains open"

## 6. Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture — Rank 0/1 Proven
- Rank 0 and 1: essentially settled
- Bhargava–Skinner–Zhang (2014): >66% of elliptic curves satisfy BSD rank conjecture
- Burns–Kakde–Kim (May 2026): proved refinement in function fields
- Rank ≥ 2: completely open, no known direct approach
- Clay workshop at Oxford scheduled for Sept 2026
