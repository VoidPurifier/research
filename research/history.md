# Obscure History

## The Dancing Plague of 1518
- In Strasbourg, a woman named Frau Troffea began dancing uncontrollably in the street
- She danced for days on swollen, bleeding feet
- Within a week, 30 others joined her. By August, ~400 people were caught up
- City council built stages and hired musicians, believing more dancing was the cure
- ~15 people died per day from exhaustion, heart attacks, and strokes
- Cause debated: ergot poisoning (hallucinogenic fungus on rye) or mass psychogenic illness

## The Great Stork Derby (1926-1936)
- Toronto lawyer Charles Millar left a will provision: whoever gave birth to the most children in the decade after his death would inherit his estate (~$9M CAD today)
- Four families tied with 9 children each, splitting the prize
- Sparked national debate, legal battles over whether stillborn or illegitimate children counted
- Took a serious physical toll on the mothers involved

## The Cadaver Synod of 897 AD
- Pope Stephen VI exhumed the corpse of Pope Formosus (dead for 7 months)
- Dressed it in papal robes and put the corpse on trial
- A deacon answered questions on behalf of the dead pope
- Found guilty of perjury
- Body stripped of vestments, thrown into the Tiber River
- Papal acts declared void

## The Voynich Manuscript
- Discovered in 1912
- 15th-century codex written in an unknown language with undeciphered script
- Contains bizarre illustrations of unidentified plants and astrological symbols
- Top codebreakers including WWII cryptographers haven't cracked it
- Some believe it contains ancient knowledge; others call it an elaborate hoax

## Woolly Mammoths vs. the Pyramids
- Small population of woolly mammoths survived on Wrangel Island (Arctic Ocean, off Siberia)
- Survived until roughly 1,700 BC — about 1,000 years after the Great Pyramid of Giza was built
- Egyptian pyramids and living mammoths were contemporaries
